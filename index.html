Conclusion and Analysis

The data show that immigration in Finland is highly diverse, both in origin and in outcomes. Some migrant groups display high employment rates and low reliance on social benefits, while others face greater challenges with unemployment and integration. Crime suspect rates also differ, but these figures are shaped by many factors, including age structure, urban concentration, and reporting practices. It is important to approach the numbers with nuance and awareness of context. Our goal is to present these realities transparently, without bias or prejudice, supporting an informed and respectful discussion about the role of immigration in Finnish society.
